# Brussels sprouts with onion and bacon
## Notes
Partial bacon packages can be stored, wrapped, in the freezer
## Utensils 
- Kitchen knife
- Spatula or slotted spoon
- Skillet
- Mixing bowl (2x or clean it after coating sprouts) 
- Baking tray
- Liner (foil, paper)
- Optional: tongs for stirring onions
## Ingredients  
- 12-20 Brussels sprouts
- 1 large onion
- 4-6 cloves of garlic or garlic powder 
- 1/3 package bacon
- Cooking oil 
- White vinegar
- Salt
- Pepper
- Onion powder

## Recipe
1. Heat oven to 400 (non-convection) 375 (convection)
2. Smash and roughly chop the garlic, if using, set aside
3. Trim and halve Brussels sprouts and add to large mixing bowl 
4. Halve onion and cut into thin slices, set aside
5. Add oil to the Brussels sprouts and mix, adding enough to coat all sprout halves
6. Add salt, pepper, onion powder, and garlic powder, mix to cover
7. Place Brussels sprouts cut-side up on a tray lined with parchment paper (alt. aluminum foil)
8. Place Brussels sprouts in the over for 20-30 minutes
9. Set skillet on medium high heat
10. Cut bacon (frozen is easier) into small pieces
11. Once you've finished cutting the bacon, add it all to the pan
12. Cook to done
13. Remove bacon, leaving fat and fond 
14. Add onions to bacon fat and cook until brown
15. If using garlic, add for the last few minutes with the onions
16. Reduce heat for the pan
17. Add ~.25 cups of white vinegar to the pan and deglaze 
18. Add salt, pepper, onion powder, optional garlic powder if you aren't adding chopped garlic, mix
19. Remove sprouts from oven when done
20. Add bacon to skillet  and mix


Consider:
- Too much vinegar taste? Add a small amount of sugar
- Serve on rice or other starch
